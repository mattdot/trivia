# Answer-domain catalog

Right-sized enumerable answer sets (~30–100 named items the solver can recite),
and the **connector** — the question pattern that routes an arbitrary *subject*
onto that answer set. Pick a subject, pick a connector, and you have a
prune-and-bet question whose answer is walkable. Rotate domains across a round
(don't lock on countries). Sizes are rough.

The mechanic: the *subject* can be anything (even unbounded — a single animal,
song, war); the *answer* must land in one of these sets. "Which animal is
fastest?" (unbounded) → route through a domain → "which **country** has the most
cheetahs?" / "in which **decade** was the cheetah's speed first measured?"

---

## Tier 1 — Universal routers (connect to almost any topic)

These three answer the **where / when / how-many** of nearly anything.

- **Countries the average person can name (~100)** — *"which country has the most / highest / first / only ___?"* — animals, crops, sports titles, inventions, disasters, foods, languages, resources, religions, brands. The single most versatile domain.
- **U.S. states (50)** — *"which state leads in / is the most ___?"* — weather, agriculture, industry, sports, demographics, food origins, parks, wildlife. (Use for any U.S.-distributed subject.)
- **U.S. presidents (46)** — *"who was president when ___ happened?"* routes **any** dated U.S.-era event (wars, inventions, laws, disasters, songs, sports firsts) onto a 46-item set — plus president attributes (age, height, prior job, home state, era).
- **Decades (~13, 1900s→2020s)** — *"in which decade did ___ happen / become popular?"* — any historical, cultural, technological, fashion, or musical development.
- **Months of the year (12)** — *"in which month does ___ happen?"* — holidays, weather extremes, sports seasons, astronomical events, historical dates. (Small but familiar; fine in moderation.)
- **The number line / guessable counts (a reasoned range)** — *"how many ___?"* — anything countable you can ballpark by reasoning: countries bordering X, moons of a planet, ingredients, players on a team, keys on a piano, bones in the foot. The "set" is the plausible numeric range; reasoning narrows it.

## Tier 2 — Geography beyond countries/states

- **Major world cities (~60 famous)** — population, elevation, latitude, weather, landmarks, hosting, cost of living.
- **U.S. national parks (63)** — size, visitors, elevation, wildlife, founding.
- **Famous mountains / rivers / islands / deserts / lakes (~40 each)** — physical superlatives, and "in which country."
- **European countries (~44)** / **African countries (~54)** — tighter regional routing when "all countries" is too broad.
- **Oceans & seas, continents (small, ~5–7)** — use sparingly; usually too small.

## Tier 3 — People & time (bounded rosters)

- **British monarchs since 1066 (~42)** — reign length, age, house, era; "who reigned when ___."
- **Greek/Roman gods (~30)** — *"which god rules ___?"* — domains, symbols, family.
- **Signers / world leaders of an era / Nobel laureates in one field+decade** — bound a famous-but-large group down to right size.
- **Zodiac signs (12), Chinese zodiac animals (12)** — small; "which sign / which year-animal."

## Tier 4 — Sports

- **A league's teams: NFL (32), NBA (30), MLB (30), NHL (32), Premier League (20), top European football clubs (~30)** — titles, city, founding, stadium, colors.
- **Olympic host cities (~30)** / **World Cup hosts** — "which host city / country ___."
- **Event venues: golf courses that have hosted majors (~50), racetracks, famous stadiums** — *"which venue has the highest / hardest / oldest ___?"* (the Oakmont pattern — widens a too-small "which tournament" into a rich set).

## Tier 5 — Culture, brands & everyday

- **Popular candies (~40), car brands (~40), fast-food chains (~30), soda brands (~25), cereals** — sales, origin, age, ingredients.
- **Currencies (~40 famous), languages (~40 major), musical instruments (~40), music genres (~30)** — "which currency/language/instrument ___."
- **Common colors (~24)** — *"what color is / represents / has the most ___?"* — flags, signals, nature, chemistry, wavelength, sports.
- **Monopoly board spaces (40), a deck of cards (52)** — fun niche sets for self-referential or probability questions.

## Tier 6 — Science & body

- **Chemical elements (all 118; famous ~40)** — properties, abundance, uses, discovery.
- **Human bones / organs / body parts (recitable ~40)** — *"which body part is the ___?"* size, count, function.
- **Constellations (88; zodiac 12), dog breeds (~40 famous), fruits / vegetables / spices (~40 each)** — superlatives and "which one is ___."

## Tier 7 — Language & symbols

- **The alphabet — 26 letters** — *"which letter begins the most ___ / is most common in ___ / is worth the most in Scrabble?"* — country names, elements, given names.
- **Greek alphabet (24), NATO phonetic alphabet (26), Roman numerals** — naming, frequency, "which letter stands for ___."

## Tier 8 — Entertainment (right-sized canons & franchises)

- **Disney animated canon (~60), Pixar films (~28), Studio Ghibli (~25)** — release order, box office, setting.
- **James Bond films (~25), MCU films (~35), Star Wars films (~12)** — order, actor, setting, box office.
- **Best Picture winners (bound by decade), a Rock Hall class, one artist's albums (~12)** — bound large catalogs by era/artist to hit right size.

---

## How to use

1. Pick the **subject** (topic du jour) and a measurable **dimension**.
2. Choose a **connector** that lands the answer in a right-sized domain — prefer where/when/how-many for unbounded subjects.
3. Check the domain is ~30–100 and **walkable** (the solver can recite candidates); widen if it's a handful ("which major" → "which venue").
4. **Rotate** domains across the round so it isn't all-countries (cluster rule).
5. Confirm the question still demands *reasoning* to prune — a right-sized set is necessary, not sufficient.
